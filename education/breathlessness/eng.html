Position & Breathing

1. Comfortable Position

Help the patient into a comfortable position (usually sitting or leaning forward).

2. Breathing Technique

Stay calm. Inhale (Count 1,2). Pursed lip breathing (like blowing a straw). Exhale (Count 1,2,3,4).

Environment

3. Ventilation

Ensure good air circulation. Open windows for fresh air.

4. Fan Therapy

Use a handheld or table fan to blow air gently towards the patient's face.

Oxygen Therapy

5. Oxygen Concentrator

Use oxygen concentrator if available/prescribed.

*Note: Not everyone benefits from oxygen therapy.

Medication

6
6. Administer 1st Dose
Aq Morphine (Liquid) OR Injection

If breathlessness persists, take Morphine as prescribed. Record the time.

7
7. After 1 Hour

If symptoms are not relieved after 1 hour:

Take 2nd Dose Morphine

Record the time of administration.

8
8. After Another 1 Hour

If symptoms persist:

Take 3rd Dose Morphine

Record time and inform your palliative care team in charge immediately.

Breathlessness Management Protocol

Home Care Instructions

Non-Pharmacological Measures

1. Position

Help the patient into a comfortable position (usually sitting upright).

2. Breathing

Stay calm. Inhale (Count 1,2). Pursed lip breathing. Exhale (Count 1,2,3,4).

3. Ventilation

Ensure good air circulation around the patient's room.

4. Fan Therapy

Use a fan to blow air gently across the patient's face.

Oxygen & Medication

5. Oxygen Concentrator

Use if available. (Note: Not everyone benefits from oxygen).

6. Administer 1st Dose

If breathless, take Liquid Morphine or Injection. Record time.

7. After 1 Hour

If not relieved, take 2nd Dose of Morphine. Record time.

8. After Another 1 Hour

If not relieved, take 3rd Dose. inform your palliative care team in charge immediately.
