🩹 Fentanyl Patch

Usage & Safety Guide

Back
Share

Application Site

Used to relieve chronic pain (e.g., cancer pain). Apply to flat skin on the Chest, Thigh, or Arm.

  • Clean skin with water only (make sure it is dry).
  • DO NOT shave hair! Cut hair short with scissors if necessary.
  • Avoid soaps, oils, or lotions on the application area.
🔥 HEAT WARNING
Avoid exposing the patch site to direct heat (hot baths, saunas, heating pads) as it can dangerously increase the release of the medicine into your body.

How to Use

1
Preparation

Tear the packet open. Fold the patch slightly to peel off half of the liner. Do not touch the adhesive side.

2
Apply

Stick the exposed part onto the skin. Peel off the remaining liner and stick the whole patch. Press firmly with your palm for 30 seconds.

3
After Care

Wash hands immediately. Write the date/time on the patch (or Tegaderm). Change every 72 hours (3 days).

DO NOT drink alcohol or drive until you know how this medicine affects your drowsiness.

If Half Dose is Needed

✂️ Cut DIAGONALLY

If the doctor prescribes a half dose, cut the patch diagonally. DO NOT cut along the existing straight line.

Safe Disposal

⚠️ DANGER: CHILDREN & PETS
Used patches still contain medicine and can be fatal if swallowed or stuck to their skin.
  1. Fold the used patch in half (sticky side inwards).
  2. Place it back into the original packet.
  3. Dispose of it in a bin that is out of reach of children.

Side Effects & Help

Common: Drowsiness, nausea, constipation, skin irritation, dizziness.

🚑 Seek Emergency Help If:
  • Slow or shallow breathing.
  • Severe drowsiness or confusion.
  • Inability to wake up or signs of overdose.
