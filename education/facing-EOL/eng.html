Facing the End of Life

A Guide to Symptoms, Care & Treatment

Physical Changes & Care

Eating & Drinking

Interest in food decreases. Swallowing may become difficult or cause coughing.

Caregiver Support:
  • Offer small sips or ice chips.
  • Use lip balm for dry lips.
  • Do not force food; this is natural.
Medical Treatment:

Meds can be given to reduce nausea or vomiting.

Breathing Changes

Breathing may become shallow, irregular (pauses), or "rattly" due to throat secretions.

Caregiver Support:
  • Raise the head of the bed (pillows).
  • Use a fan for gentle air flow.
  • "Rattling" is rarely painful; turning usually helps.
Medical Treatment:

Opioids (for breathlessness) or Anticholinergics (to dry up secretions).

Mind, Sleep & Agitation

Drowsiness, confusion, or seeing things not there (delirium). Restlessness may occur.

Caregiver Support:
  • Speak calmly; hearing is the last sense to go.
  • Keep room peaceful with soft lighting.
  • Avoid restraints; hold hands instead.
Medical Treatment:

Sedatives may be prescribed to reduce agitation and anxiety.

Skin & Temperature

Hands/feet may feel cold, pale, or mottled. Fever can also occur.

Caregiver Support:
  • Use soft, warm blankets.
  • Reposition gently every few hours.
  • Apply lotions for moisture.
Urine & Bowels

Urine decreases and darkens. Incontinence or constipation may occur.

Caregiver Support:
  • Keep area clean; use pads/sheets.
  • Maintain hygiene for comfort.
Medical Treatment:

Laxatives for constipation; catheter care if required.

Pain Management

Pain may continue or increase, or appear as restlessness.

Caregiver Support:
  • Watch for grimacing or moaning.
  • Give extra doses (PRN) if instructed.
Medical Treatment:

Regular pain relief (via pump or patch) adjusted by doctor.

Administration

Meds are used for comfort, not to change the timing of death.

Syringe Driver / Pump

A small battery pump delivers meds continuously under the skin (subcutaneous).

Patches & Buccal

Skin patches (e.g., Fentanyl) or meds placed inside the cheek for fast absorption.

Important: Do not change medicines yourself. Always follow the palliative care team's plan.

After Death Occurs

Immediate Moments

There is no rush. Spend time with your loved one. Sit quietly, pray, or say goodbye.

What to do

  • Call 999: Inform them of the death so a doctor can verify it.
  • Funeral Director: Contact them once death is verified.
