What is MBO?

Malignant Bowel Obstruction (MBO) occurs when the intestines become blocked, usually due to advanced cancer. This blockage stops food, fluids, and gas from passing through normally.

Note: It can be caused by the tumor itself, constipation, or scar tissue from previous surgeries. This guide helps you manage the symptoms at home.

Common Symptoms
  • Abdominal pain (Cramping)
  • Swollen abdomen (Distention)
  • Nausea & Vomiting
  • Cannot pass gas or stool

When Symptoms Recur

If symptoms return at home, follow these steps:

1
Bowel Rest (Fasting)

Stop solid foods for 12-24 hours. You may take small sips of clear fluids if thirsty. This gives your bowel a break.

2
If Improving...

Gradually restart with liquids, then puree, then soft foods (e.g., porridge). Stick to a Low-Fibre Diet.

3
If Worsening...

If pain persists, vomiting continues, or you have a fever, contact your doctor or hospice nurse immediately.

Low-Fibre Diet

A low-fibre diet produces less waste/bulk, making it easier for your bowel to handle.

Carbohydrates
  • White bread
  • White rice / Porridge
  • Peeled potatoes
Protein
  • Fish
  • Eggs / Tofu
  • Soft cooked meat
  • Milk / Cheese
Fruits & Veg
  • Cooked carrots/pumpkin
  • Melon / Papaya
  • No skins or seeds
Avoid
  • Whole grains
  • Raw vegetables
  • Nuts & Seeds
  • Stringy foods

Malignant Bowel Obstruction

Patient Self Care Guide

What is MBO?

Malignant Bowel Obstruction (MBO) occurs when the intestines become blocked, usually due to advanced cancer. This stops food, fluids, and gas from passing through normally.

Common Symptoms

  • Abdominal Pain (Cramps)
  • Distention (Swollen tummy)
  • Vomiting / Nausea
  • Inability to pass gas or stool

Action Plan: When symptoms recur

1. Bowel Rest

Fast for 12-24 hours. Only small sips of clear fluid.

2. Restarting Diet

If better, start liquids -> puree -> soft foods. Low Fibre only.

Low-Fibre Diet Guide

Allowed Foods
  • White rice, white bread
  • Fish, eggs, tofu, soft meat
  • Peeled potatoes, cooked carrots
  • Melon, papaya, clear juice
Foods to Avoid
  • Whole grains, brown rice
  • Raw vegetables, salad
  • Fruit skins, seeds, nuts
  • Stringy/tough meats
Medical Alert: If pain is severe, vomiting persists, or you have a fever, seek medical attention immediately.
