Understanding Bleeding

What is it?

Bleeding can happen from the mouth, nose, skin wounds, tumour sites, lungs, stomach or bowel in advanced illness.

It may be minor and slow, or sudden and heavy. Management aims to reduce distress, maintain comfort, and respect the person’s priorities.

Immediate Actions

1. Stay Calm & Be There

Stay with the patient. Reassure them using a calm voice and touch. The priority is to simply be there.

2. Use Dark Towels

Use dark towels to disguise the visibility of blood and reduce distress for everyone present.

Medication Protocol

1
Administer Sedation
Buccal Midazolam / Lorazepam

Administer buccal Midazolam or Buccal Lorazepam to keep the patient calm.

2
For Breathlessness
Morphine / Opioid

If the patient feels breathless, give the prescribed dose of as-needed morphine/opioid.

Seek Help

Contact your palliative care team in charge.

About Palliative Bleeding

Bleeding can happen from the mouth, nose, skin wounds, tumour sites, or lungs. It may be minor or sudden. Management aims to reduce distress and maintain comfort.

1. Stay Calm & Be There

The priority is to stay with the patient. Reassure them using a calm voice and comforting touch.

2. Use Dark Towels

Use dark towels to disguise the visibility of blood. This significantly reduces distress.

Medication Protocol

1. Administer Sedation

Administer buccal Midazolam or Buccal Lorazepam to keep the patient calm.

2. Treat Breathlessness

If patient feels breathless, give the prescribed dose of as-needed morphine/opioid.

3. Seek Help

Contact your palliative care team in charge.
