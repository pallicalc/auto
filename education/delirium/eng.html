What is Delirium?

Confusion & Restlessness

Sudden change in the brain. Not knowing time/place, mixing up day/night, or agitation.

Hallucinations

Seeing or hearing things not there. Picking at the air. Can be fearful for the patient.

Home Care Steps

1
Keep Calm

Speak slowly and gently. Reassure frequently. Reduce noise and bright lights.

2
Orientate

Use clocks, calendars, and familiar items. Gently remind the patient of the time and place.

Triggers & Meds

3
Check Triggers

Is the patient in pain? Are they constipated? Are they unable to pass urine?

4
Medication Protocol

Administer prescribed medication (Buccal/Subcut). Repeat every 1 hour if required.

Urgent Help

Contact Palliative Care Team

Call immediately if:

  • Restlessness/Agitation is not resolved.
  • There is a risk of harm or falls.
  • Patient develops fever or breathing issues.

Terminal Delirium Management

Home Care & Symptom Guide

Definition & Signs

Confusion & Agitation

Sudden brain change. Not knowing time/place, mixing day/night, climbing out of bed.

Hallucinations

Seeing/hearing things not there. Picking at air. Fearfulness.

Care Steps

1
Calm & Reassure

Speak slowly. Reduce noise. Use soft lights.

2
Orientate

Use familiar items, clocks, and calendars to remind patient of surroundings.

3
Check Triggers

Check for PAIN, CONSTIPATION, or URINE RETENTION.

4
Safety

Clear walkways. Do not leave alone if unsafe.

Medication

💊
Administer as Prescribed

Give medication via Buccal or Subcut route. Repeat every 1 hr if needed.

Urgent

Contact Palliative Care Team If:

- Agitation is unresolved.

- Risk of harm to self or others.

- New fever or distress.
